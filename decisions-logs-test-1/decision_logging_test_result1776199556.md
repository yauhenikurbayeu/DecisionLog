## Context

An AI-powered document platform must select its next-quarter focus. Four candidate initiatives exist: AI contract generation, analytics dashboard, workflow automation, and real-time collaboration improvements. Key environmental factors:

- **Customer trust deficit:** Customers report low trust in AI outputs, making AI-forward features risky to ship.
- **Sales pressure:** Sales team pushes for AI-branded features to drive pipeline.
- **Engineering capacity:** Engineering is overloaded with tech debt, limiting how much new scope can be absorbed.
- **Competitive threat:** A competitor recently launched workflow automation, creating urgency to respond.

## Options Evaluated

| Option | Assessment |
|---|---|
| A) AI contract generation | High sales appeal but directly conflicts with the customer trust deficit. Launching an AI-heavy feature into a low-trust environment risks poor adoption, negative feedback loops, and further erosion of credibility. Rejected as primary or secondary. |
| B) Analytics dashboard | Low engineering cost, improves platform transparency, and supports trust recovery by giving users visibility into AI outputs and usage patterns. Strong secondary candidate. |
| C) Workflow automation | Directly responds to the competitor's automation launch. Can be scoped with deterministic, rule-based components to limit AI-trust exposure. Moderate engineering lift but high strategic value. Strong primary candidate. |
| D) Real-time collaboration improvements | Foundational platform investment that benefits all future features, but does not address the immediate competitive threat or the trust deficit. Deferred to a future quarter. |

## Final Decision

- **Primary initiative:** **Workflow automation** — responds to the competitive threat, can be delivered with rule-based components that sidestep the AI trust issue, and delivers concrete customer value.
- **Secondary initiative:** **Analytics dashboard** — low engineering footprint, provides transparency into AI behavior and platform usage, and directly supports trust recovery without adding to tech debt.
