## Context

An AI-powered document platform must choose its next-quarter focus. Key environmental factors:

- **Customer trust deficit**: Users report low confidence in AI-generated outputs, creating adoption friction for any AI-heavy feature.
- **Sales pressure**: The go-to-market team is pushing for more AI capabilities to differentiate in demos and close deals.
- **Engineering capacity constraints**: The team carries significant tech debt, limiting bandwidth for large greenfield initiatives.
- **Competitive move**: A direct competitor has shipped workflow automation, creating market pressure to respond.

## Options Evaluated

| Option | Description |
|---|---|
| A) AI contract generation | New AI-powered feature for generating contracts from templates and context |
| B) Analytics dashboard | Reporting and insights layer over document activity and usage |
| C) Workflow automation | Rule-based and AI-assisted document routing, approvals, and triggers |
| D) Real-time collaboration improvements | Enhancements to multi-user editing, presence, and co-authoring experience |

## Final Decision

**Primary initiative: D) Real-time collaboration improvements**

**Secondary initiative: C) Workflow automation**
