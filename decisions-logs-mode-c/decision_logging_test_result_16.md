Rationale: "Limited Beta Rollout Plan" accurately signals the constrained, controlled nature of the release without being overly internal (as "Design-Partner Rollout Plan" might read) or too vague (as "Launch Preparation Note" would be). It sets clear expectations for any reader about the scope.

## Memo Draft

# Limited Beta Rollout Plan

**Date:** 2026-04-21
**Status:** Approved for execution

### Overview

This memo outlines the initial rollout of the summarization feature under a strictly limited beta arrangement. The goal is to validate quality and process before any broader release.

### Approved Rollout Scope

- The feature will be released to **one design partner only**. No additional participants will be onboarded during this phase.
- **Every generated summary requires manual human review** prior to delivery. Automated or unreviewed outputs must not reach the partner.

### Risks

- Single-partner feedback may not surface edge cases representative of a broader user base.
- Manual review creates a throughput bottleneck; resourcing must be confirmed before go-live.
- Delays in review could erode partner trust if turnaround expectations are not set clearly upfront.

### Next Steps

1. Confirm design partner onboarding date and point of contact.
2. Assign and brief the human review team; establish SLA for review turnaround.
3. Define escalation path for summaries that fail review.
4. Schedule a mid-beta checkpoint (suggested: two weeks post-launch) to assess quality and process health.
5. Document findings for use in scoping a broader rollout decision.
