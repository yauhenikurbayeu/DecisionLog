## Context

An AI-powered document platform must choose its next-quarter priorities under four competing pressures: customers report low trust in AI outputs, sales demands AI features, engineering is overloaded with tech debt, and a competitor just launched workflow automation.

## Options Evaluated

| Option | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| A) AI Contract Generation | Strong AI narrative for sales; differentiation | High adoption risk given low customer trust; heavy engineering lift |
| B) Analytics Dashboard | Directly addresses trust deficit; moderate engineering scope | Not competitively urgent; perceived as non-exciting |
| C) Workflow Automation | Responds to competitor threat; scopeable as rule-based (sidesteps trust issue); satisfies sales partially | Adds load on debt-burdened engineering; does not resolve trust |
| D) Real-time Collaboration | Quality-of-life improvement | Low strategic alignment with any pressing constraint |

## Final Decision

**Primary initiative: C) Workflow Automation**

**Secondary initiative: B) Analytics Dashboard**
