## Context

An AI-powered document platform must select its primary and secondary initiatives for the next quarter. Four competing pressures shape the decision: customers report low trust in AI outputs, sales demands AI features, engineering is overloaded with tech debt, and a competitor just launched workflow automation.

## Options Evaluated

- **A) AI contract generation** — Highest sales appeal but highest adoption risk given the trust deficit. Pushing generative AI output at customers who already distrust AI is likely to deepen the problem, not resolve it.
- **B) Analytics dashboard** — Directly addresses the root cause of the trust problem by giving customers visibility into AI behavior and activity metrics. Moderate engineering lift, well-understood scope. Creates foundational infrastructure that compounds value for every future AI feature.
- **C) Workflow automation** — Responds to the competitor's launch. Can be scoped as rule-based (not generative AI), partially sidestepping trust concerns. Strong sales narrative. However, complex to deliver cleanly under existing tech debt, and does not fix the underlying trust ceiling.
- **D) Real-time collaboration improvements** — Does not address the competitive threat, trust problem, or sales pressure. Lowest strategic urgency this quarter.

## Final Decision

- **Primary initiative: B) Analytics dashboard**
- **Secondary initiative: C) Workflow automation**
